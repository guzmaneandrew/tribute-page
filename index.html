The Notorious B.I.G.

Here's a timeline of The Notorious B.I.G.'s life:

  • 1972: Wallace was born in the Brooklyn borough of New York City on May 21, 1972, the only child of Jamaican immigrant parents.
  • 1972–1991: He began rapping as a teenager, entertaining people on the streets, and performed with local groups. In his adolescent years, he became more involved in crime. In 1991, Wallace was arrested for dealing crack cocaine and spent nine months in jail.
  • 1991–1994: Sean "Puffy" Combs signed Wallace to Uptown after hearing his demo tape in 1992. A year later, Puffy was fired from Uptown and launched Bad Boy Records, instantly Wallace's new label. On August 8, 1993, Wallace's first child, T'yanna was born. Although he continued dealing drugs, Combs discovered that and obliged him to quit.
  • 1994: Wallace had his first pop chart success as a solo artist with double A-side, "Juicy / Unbelievable". Ready to Die was released on September 13, 1994 and reached No. 13 on the Billboard 200 chart. Around the time of the album's release, Wallace became friends with fellow rapper Tupac Shakur.
  • 1995: In August 1995, Wallace's protégé group, Junior M.A.F.I.A., released their debut album Conspiracy. The record went Gold and its singles, "Player's Anthem" and "Get Money", both featuring Wallace, went Gold and Platinum. Wallace continued to work with R&B artists, by the end of the year, he was the top-selling male solo artist and rapper on the U.S. pop and R&B charts. In his year of success, Wallace became involved in a rivalry between the East and West Coast hip hop scenes with Shakur, now his former friend.
  • 1996: Wallace worked with pop singer Michael Jackson on the album HIStory. On September 7, 1996, Shakur was shot multiple times in a drive-by shooting and died. Rumors of Wallace's involvement with Shakur's murder spread. On October 29, 1996, Faith Evans gave birth to Wallace's son, Christopher "C.J." Wallace Jr.
  • 1997: During the recording for his second album, Life After Death, Wallace and Lil' Cease were in a car accident. Despite his hospitalization, he continued to work on the album. On March 9, 1997, Wallace was returning to his hotel after a party and was shot to death.
